# Trust and Safety

## Purpose

This document explains how Taja should protect users, sellers, and the platform from abuse or unsafe behavior.

## Safety Goals

| Goal | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Prevent abuse | Stop spam, fraud, and harmful behavior |
| Protect users | Keep personal and financial actions safe |
| Protect sellers | Reduce fake orders, chargeback risk, and impersonation |
| Protect operations | Give admins control and visibility |

## Risk Areas

| Risk | Example |
|---|---|
| Fake products | Listings that misrepresent goods |
| Fraudulent sellers | Sellers who abuse payouts or listings |
| Fake customers | Suspicious orders or spam |
| Account takeover | Unauthorized access to a user account |
| Payment disputes | Conflicting payment or delivery claims |
| Data abuse | Misuse of customer or seller information |

## Safety Controls

| Control | Purpose |
|---|---|
| Verification | Check seller or user identity when needed |
| Moderation | Review content before or after publication |
| Rate limiting | Reduce spam and abuse |
| Audit logs | Track important actions |
| Escalation paths | Route serious issues to humans |
| Deletion flows | Allow account removal requests safely |

## Moderation States

| State | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Approved | Content or user can operate normally |
| Pending review | Needs human review before proceeding |
| Rejected | Not allowed on the platform |
| Restricted | Limited access due to risk |
| Suspended | Temporarily blocked |

## Summary

Trust and safety should be built into the product rather than handled only after problems appear.
