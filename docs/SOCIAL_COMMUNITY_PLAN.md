# Social and Community Plan

## Purpose

This document defines how Taja should show up across social platforms and community channels.
It is meant to help marketing, product, support, and community teams work from one consistent plan.

## Goals

| Goal | What Success Looks Like |
|---|---|
| Awareness | People discover Taja across social channels |
| Trust | The product looks active, useful, and real |
| Education | Audiences understand what Taja does and why it matters |
| Community | Users have a place to ask questions and give feedback |
| Conversion | Social traffic turns into signups, demos, customers, or seller leads |

## Channel Strategy

| Channel | Primary Role | Audience | Content Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| X | Fast updates, product progress, opinion, launches | Builders, founders, tech audience | Short updates, threads, clips |
| Instagram | Visual brand building and product storytelling | Customers, creators, young founders | Carousels, reels, screenshots |
| Facebook | Community reach and broad local awareness | General audience, local businesses | Posts, testimonials, videos |
| LinkedIn | Business credibility and hiring confidence | Professionals, partners, investors | Product updates, milestones, insights |
| Telegram | Community discussion and support | Users, early adopters, sellers | Topics, announcements, support |
| Discord | Deeper builder and power-user community | Builders, testers, contributors | Categories, feedback, support, updates |

## Posting Cadence

| Channel | Posts Per Day | Notes |
|---|---:|---|
| X | 2 to 4 | Can include one thread or product update |
| Instagram | 1 to 2 | Focus on visual posts or reels |
| Facebook | 1 | Good for announcements and community reach |
| LinkedIn | 1 | Keep it professional and insight-driven |
| Telegram | 1 to 3 | Mix announcements and discussion prompts |
| Discord | 1 to 2 | Keep the server active without noise |

## What We Post

| Content Type | Example | Best Channels |
|---|---|---|
| Product updates | New flow, feature, release note | X, LinkedIn, Telegram |
| Educational content | How Taja works, how WhatsApp commerce works | Instagram, LinkedIn, Facebook |
| Launch content | New page, new flow, new milestone | X, Instagram, LinkedIn |
| Proof content | Screenshots, testimonials, usage stats | X, Instagram, Facebook, LinkedIn |
| Community prompts | Questions, polls, feedback requests | Telegram, Discord, X |
| Behind-the-scenes | Build progress, team notes, lessons learned | X, LinkedIn, Discord |

## Content Pillars

| Pillar | Description |
|---|---|
| Product | What Taja does and what is shipping next |
| Proof | Screenshots, demos, testimonials, metrics |
| Education | Explaining commerce, AI, and WhatsApp workflows |
| Community | Questions, polls, replies, and feedback loops |
| Trust | Security, reliability, receipts, and support clarity |

## Weekly Rhythm

| Day | Focus |
|---|---|
| Monday | Product roadmap and launch planning |
| Tuesday | Feature explanation and education |
| Wednesday | Proof, screenshots, or demo content |
| Thursday | Community questions and feedback |
| Friday | Business updates, milestones, or recap |
| Saturday | Light community content and social proof |
| Sunday | Planning, reflections, or softer brand content |

## Operating Rules

| Rule | Why It Matters |
|---|---|
| Keep the message consistent across channels | Prevent confusion |
| Reuse the same core story in different formats | Save time and improve clarity |
| Tailor depth to the channel | X should be shorter than LinkedIn |
| Avoid random posting | Keep the brand credible and intentional |
| Include calls to action | Help posts drive signups, replies, or community growth |

| LinkedIn | High | Build credibility and business trust |

## Summary

Taja should present itself as an active, credible, and community-driven product.

The social plan should help the team:

- explain the product clearly
- build trust with users and partners
- keep a steady public presence
- collect feedback from the community
- turn attention into users and revenue
