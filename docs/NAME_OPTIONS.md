# Name Options

## Why The Name Matters

A good name should be:

- easy to say
- easy to remember
- connected to commerce or growth
- flexible enough for product expansion
- strong enough for brand, product, and future marketing

## Current Name

| Name | Meaning | Why It Works |
|---|---|---|
| Taja | Yoruba for "sell" | Directly tied to commerce and action |

## Alternative Name Ideas

| # | Name | Positioning | Notes |
|---:|---|---|---|
| 1 | Taara | Direct, simple, modern | Sounds close to "trade" and is easy to brand |
| 2 | Sowo | Selling and exchange | Short, memorable, commerce-focused |
| 3 | Kasuwa | Marketplace energy | Strong local-market feel |
| 4 | Mercha | Merchant identity | Modern and product-friendly |
| 5 | Sellsy | Sales-first platform | Clear, direct, and startup-like |
| 6 | Trada | Trade and business flow | Good for a broader commerce platform |
| 7 | Vendo | Selling and storefronts | Short and easy to remember |
| 8 | NairaHub | Small business commerce | Strong Nigerian context, finance-adjacent |
| 9 | Shoplane | Shopping and discovery | Good for customer-facing commerce |
| 10 | Marketa | Marketplace and retail | Broad enough for growth and expansion |

## Shortlisting Criteria

| Criterion | What We Want |
|---|---|
| Brand fit | The name should reflect commerce and trust |
| Memorability | People should remember it after hearing it once or twice |
| Flexibility | It should work for future product lines and features |
| Cultural fit | It should feel natural for the target audience |
| Availability | It should be practical for domains, social handles, and app naming |

## Recommendation

If the team wants to keep the strongest local identity, keep **Taja**.

If the team wants a more neutral international product name, shortlist:

- Taara
- Sowo
- Trada
- Vendo
- Marketa

These five keep the product in the commerce space while giving more naming flexibility for future branding.
