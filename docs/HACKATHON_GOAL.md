# Hackathon Goal

## Why We Are Building Taja For This Hackathon

Taja is our submission for the Build with Gemini XPRIZE because it solves a real-world problem with real business value:

- small businesses need a simpler way to sell online
- customers need a less fragmented shopping experience
- sellers need easier onboarding, product management, and payouts
- support teams need fewer manual steps and faster resolution
- the business must be able to operate with AI in production, not just in a demo

Our goal is to show that a small team can build and run a real commerce business with AI as part of the day-to-day operating system.

## Hackathon Category Fit

Taja most naturally fits:

- **Small Business Services**

It also has strong overlap with:

- **Entrepreneurship & Job Creation**
- **Professional Services Access**
- **Money & Financial Access**

## What Success Means For Us

Success is not just “the app works.”

Success means:

1. real customers can use the product
2. real sellers can onboard and manage products
3. the platform can process actual commerce activity
4. AI can support discovery, support, and operational decisions
5. the product can generate or enable revenue
6. the team can prove the business is viable beyond the hackathon

## Our Core Hackathon Goal

Build a WhatsApp-native commerce business where the customer journey, seller journey, and support journey are all driven by a combination of:

- the official WhatsApp Cloud API
- AI-assisted workflows
- the multimodal model service
- product and operational dashboards

The business should feel like a real company, not a prototype that only exists for judges.

## What AI Does In The Business

AI should help with:

- product discovery from text, image, video, and voice note
- matching customers to the right products
- support triage and escalation
- seller onboarding guidance
- form completion inside WhatsApp
- duplicate detection and search relevance
- ranking and recommendations
- operational decision support

## What Humans Still Do

Humans should focus on:

- product direction
- final support escalation
- seller relationships
- business growth
- revenue tracking
- quality control
- customer trust

The intent is not to remove humans from the business.
The intent is to let AI handle the repetitive work so humans can focus on higher-value decisions.

## Why This Matters

Taja is not just a chat bot.
It is a business operating system for commerce inside WhatsApp.

That matters because it can:

- reduce friction for small businesses
- create new selling opportunities
- make support easier to access
- help users who prefer chat over websites
- lower the technical barrier to starting and running a business

## Evidence We Need To Show Judges

For the submission, we should be able to show:

- live GitHub repository
- product documentation
- working prototype
- AI execution logs
- API usage records
- screenshots of dashboards and flows
- real customer feedback
- revenue or transaction evidence
- marketing or customer acquisition spend

## Build Approach

We are building this in a way that:

- keeps the official WhatsApp experience in-app
- uses the model service for multimodal search and similarity
- keeps the backend modular and maintainable
- supports real operations, not just demo interactions
- gives the team a shared understanding of the product goal

## Final Statement

Our hackathon goal is to prove that Taja can operate as a real, AI-native commerce business that helps small businesses earn money, helps customers buy more easily, and creates a product that can continue after the hackathon ends.
